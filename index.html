Why are animals going extinct?!

Every day, wild animal species around the planet are going extinct. For each species that go extinct, many more become and remain endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and climate change. Several of animals are on the endangered list, yet still are being either poached or threatened out of their homes. Out of many articles explaining the fact that animals are going extinct humans are the reason to blame, everything includes from global expansion to a simple restaurant that isn't significantly required for wherever it would be placed. Down below are some of the few animals that are being threatened.

White Rhinoceros

Fun Fact:
There is actually no colour difference between the black and white rhinos species. The “white” component of the name may have resulted from a mistranslation of the Afrikaans word “wyd” meaning “wide”



Giant Panda

Fun Fact:
giant panda, also called the panda. Only about 1,500 of these black-and-white relatives of bears survive in the wild. Pandas eat almost nothing but bamboo shoots and leaves.



Tiger (Panthera Tigris)

Fun Fact:
A tiger's saliva is antiseptic, and comes handy when a tiger cleans its wounds.



Whooping crane(Grus americana)

Fun fact:
At five feet tall, the whooping crane is the tallest bird in North America. They are named for their whooping unison calls.



Blue Whale(Balaenoptera musculus)

Fun fact:
The blue whale eats up to 5 tons (4536 Kilograms) of krill per day.



Asian elephant(Elephas maximus)

Fun fact:
Male Asian elephants have small tusks while female Asian elephants have no tusks at all.



Sea Otter(Enhydra lutris)

Fun Fact:
Unlike other marine mammals, sea otters do not have a blubber layer.



Snow leopard(Panthera uncia)

Fun fact:
Snow leopards live at very high elevations where there are steep cliffs.



gorilla (Gorilla beringei)

Fun fact:
Ebola epidemic outbreaks across the Congo Basin are a significant threat to gorillas, and also impact the few re- maining populations less exposed to poaching and habitat loss.>



Tasmanian devil(Sarcophilus harrisii)

Fun fact:
Typically a Tasmanian devils will consume about 15% of body weight in food each day.



orangutan (Pongo pygmaeus)

Fun fact:
Orang-utans are the world's largest tree-climbing mammals.
